# The Tampa Deal Memo: "From Lab to Scale"

## The Core Narrative: Why Kelly Spicers? Why Lindenmeyr?

**The Question:** "If you're so great, why are you testing with Kelly Spicers (KS) and not us?"
**The Answer:** "Because you don't test a Ferrari engine in a semi-truck. You test it on the track first."

### **The "Lab vs. Scale" Argument**

1.  **Kelly Spicers was the Lab.**
    *   KS is agile, West Coast, and hungry. They were the perfect "Petri dish" to prove the hypothesis. We needed a controlled environment to break things, fix them, and prove the "60-Second Interaction" works without disrupting a massive ship.
    *   **The Result:** We proved the wedge. We proved the data model. We proved the ROI. The experiment is over. The science is settled.

2.  **Lindenmeyr is the Scale.**
    *   Now that the engine is built, we need a chassis big enough to handle the horsepower. That’s you.
    *   We aren't coming to you to *test*. We are coming to you to *dominate*.
    *   KS gave us the blueprint. Lindenmeyr gives us the empire.

## The Deal Structure: The $1M Development Agreement

**The Offer:**
We are closing a $1M SAFE round by year-end to bridge us to Series A. We have interest from financial investors, but we want **Strategic Capital** first.

**The "Development Deal" (The Anchor):**
*   **Investment:** Lindenmeyr commits **$1M** as a "Pre-Paid Development & Licensing Agreement" (or convertible note).
*   **The "Get":**
    *   **Exclusivity:** First-to-market access to the "Enterprise Edition" of Defft.ai for 12 months in the East/Midwest.
    *   **Influence:** A seat on the "Product Council" to prioritize features that solve *Lindenmeyr's* specific bottlenecks (e.g., specific ERP integrations, regional inventory logic).
    *   **The "Sidecar" (The Sweetener):** We are carving out a personal allocation for the executives in this room. We want you aligned with the upside, not just the software.
